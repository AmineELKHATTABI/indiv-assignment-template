Cumulative Returns: ETFs vs Futures (2019-2023)

Interpretation

This line graph illustrates the cumulative returns for GLD ETF, SLV ETF, Gold Futures, and Silver Futures from 2019 to 2023. Each line represents the growth of a $1 investment in each asset over the time period.

Discussion

The plot reveals that all assets experienced periods of growth and decline, with notable volatility around early 2020, likely due to market reactions to global events. Gold-related assets (GLD ETF and Gold Futures) moved closely together, suggesting similar market behavior, while Silver Futures showed the highest volatility.

Sources

Data sourced from Yahoo Finance via the yfinance Python package.

Annual Mean Cumulative Returns (2019-2023)

Interpretation

The bar chart presents the annual mean cumulative returns for each asset, giving a clearer comparison of average yearly performance.

Discussion

The chart highlights the differences in average returns, with some years favoring one asset over others. The analysis could be deepened with an exploration of the underlying causes of these performance disparities, such as economic conditions or market sentiment.

Annual Volatility of GLD, SLV, Gold Futures, and Silver Futures

Interpretation

This visualization shows the annual volatility, a measure of the dispersion of returns, for each investment asset.

Discussion

Silver Futures exhibit the highest volatility, indicating a riskier asset compared to the others. GLD ETF appears to be the least volatile, suggesting it may be a more stable investment choice.

Sharpe Ratio of GLD, SLV, Gold Futures, and Silver Futures

Interpretation

The Sharpe Ratio bar chart provides insight into the risk-adjusted returns of each asset, factoring in the volatility.

Discussion

Despite its high volatility, Silver Futures demonstrate a comparable Sharpe Ratio to other assets, indicating that the higher risk is counterbalanced by higher returns. This suggests that when adjusted for risk, Silver Futures' performance is competitive with the other assets.
