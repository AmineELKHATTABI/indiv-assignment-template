Cumulative Returns: ETFs vs Futures (2019-2023)

Interpretation

This line graph illustrates the cumulative returns for GLD ETF, SLV ETF, Gold Futures, and Silver Futures from 2019 to 2023. Each line represents the growth of a $1 investment in each asset over the time period.

Sources

Data sourced from Yahoo Finance via the yfinance Python package.

Annual Mean Cumulative Returns (2019-2023)

Interpretation

The bar chart depicts the annual mean cumulative returns for GLD (Gold ETF), SLV (Silver ETF), Gold Futures, and Silver Futures from 2019 to 2023. Each bar color corresponds to a different asset, allowing for an immediate visual comparison of their respective performance year over year.

Interpretation

The bar chart illustrates the annual volatility of four investment assets: GLD ETF (Gold ETF), SLV ETF (Silver ETF), Gold Futures, and Silver Futures.

Sharpe Ratio of GLD, SLV, Gold Futures, and Silver Futures

Interpretation

The bar chart displays the Sharpe Ratio which is used to calculate the return of an investment compared to its risk, for four investment assets: GLD ETF, SLV ETF, Gold Futures, and Silver Futures.

Discussion

The set of visualizations presents a comprehensive analysis of the performance and risk characteristics of two ETFs, GLD (Gold) and SLV (Silver), as well as their corresponding futures contracts, over a period extending from 2019 to 2023. The first plot, illustrating cumulative returns, provides a visual narrative of the investment journey of each asset. The synchronized movement of the GLD ETF and Gold Futures indicates that the ETF successfully mimics the behavior of the underlying commodity, offering a more accessible investment vehicle without significantly diverging from the market it tracks. The SLV ETF and Silver Futures lines also move in tandem, yet with pronounced volatility, suggesting silver's more erratic market behavior. Interestingly, the sharp rise and fall observed in early 2020 across all assets likely reflect the market's reaction to the initial impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, a period marked by high uncertainty and a rush to safe-haven assets like gold and silver. The annual mean cumulative returns bar chart shifts the focus from overall performance to a year-by-year comparison, revealing how each asset fared on average at the end of each year. Here, 2021 emerges as a particularly strong year for precious metals, possibly indicating a confluence of factors such as economic recovery optimism and inflationary pressures that often bolster the appeal of gold and silver as investment options. The variability across years also echoes the underlying complexity of the commodities market, driven by diverse factors from geopolitical tensions to supply-demand dynamics. The annual volatility bar chart further enriches the story by quantifying risk. It becomes evident that while the SLV ETF carries the most risk, as seen in its higher volatility, the GLD ETF stands out as a beacon of stability. This distinction is pivotal for portfolio construction, as investors may look to balance high-risk, high-reward assets with more stable ones. Notably, the volatilities of Gold and Silver Futures fall between the two ETFs, with Silver Futures tipping towards the higher end, likely a reflection of both the inherent price swings in silver and the leveraged nature of futures contracts. Lastly, the Sharpe Ratio bar chart synthesizes both return and risk into a single metric to evaluate the risk-adjusted performance. The GLD ETF's superior Sharpe Ratio signals a more efficient conversion of risk into return compared to the others. Despite the heightened volatility of silver, the Silver Futures demonstrate a Sharpe Ratio that holds its ground against the less volatile assets, suggesting a potential equilibrium between risk and return. This could be indicative of a market that rewards the risks associated with silver with proportionate returns. Together, these plots unveil a narrative of risk and return where GLD ETF emerges as a consistent performer, SLV ETF as the most volatile, and the futures contracts as instruments that encapsulate the complexities of leverage and market speculation.
